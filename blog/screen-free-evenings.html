Winning back your evenings: a gentle guide to less screen time

May 30, 2026 · 1 min read · Family Life

Here's the truth nobody puts on a parenting poster: screens won because they're easy — for children and for exhausted parents. Any honest plan to reduce screen time has to respect that, or it collapses by Wednesday.

Don't remove. Replace.

A screen ban creates a vacuum, and vacuums get filled with whining. What works is a competing offer that's genuinely attractive and — this is the crucial part — requires you for the fun. Children rarely choose a tablet over a parent who is actually playing.

Three swaps that actually hold

  • The dinner-table question game. One riddle or 'would you rather' card while plates are cleared. Two minutes, huge returns.
  • The 'first ten' rule. The first ten minutes after you get home are device-free for everyone — including you. A quick card game fills them perfectly.
  • The wind-down flip. Replace the pre-bed video with two quiet cards in bed. Same ritual comfort, none of the blue-light wind-up.

Expect the extinction burst

Days two through four are the hardest — protest peaks right before a habit breaks. Hold the line warmly. By the second week, most families report the protests are gone and the ritual is requested.

You're not anti-screen. You're pro-evening. There's a difference, and your child will feel it.
