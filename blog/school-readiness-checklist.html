The gentle school-readiness checklist (no drilling required)

May 12, 2026 · 1 min read · Early Years

Ask a kindergarten teacher what they wish children arrived with, and they rarely say 'reading'. They say things like: can sit with an activity for ten minutes, can name what they're feeling, can hear that 'cat' starts with c. The good news: every one of these grows beautifully in small, playful doses.

The five that matter

  1. Attention stamina. Not hours — just the ability to stay with one enjoyable task for 10–15 minutes. Card games are quiet stamina training in disguise.
  2. Sound awareness. Hearing the first sounds in words predicts reading better than knowing letter names. 'I spy something starting with sss…' is a complete curriculum.
  3. Number sense. Knowing that five objects are five, however they're arranged — built by counting real things, not reciting to 100.
  4. Feeling words. Children who can say 'I'm frustrated' don't have to show you instead. Emotion cards make these words normal dinner conversation.
  5. Confidence with not-knowing. The child who shrugs happily and guesses is ready for a classroom. Celebrate brave wrong answers loudly.

What to skip

Worksheets before age five. Flash-drilling under time pressure. Anything that makes a four-year-old feel tested. Readiness built on anxiety is a loan the child repays later.

Ten playful minutes a day, across a summer, covers this entire list. That's the whole secret.
